Overview

The Squeaky Wheel is a shortwave radio station transmitting at 5367 kHz USB (daytime voice) and 3363.5 kHz USB (nighttime voice). Known for its distinctive squeaky sound, it has been broadcasting since the early 1980s and occasionally includes voice messages, often in Russian.

History

The station first surfaced in the 1980s and is believed to be linked to the Russian military. Its continuous squeaky signal has led to speculation regarding its purpose, which many theorize is related to military communications or signals to operatives.

Functionality

The Squeaky Wheel is thought to serve a signaling function, though the specifics remain uncertain. The clarity and strength of its transmissions can vary, depending on atmospheric conditions, which affects listener reception.

Anecdotes

Amateur radio enthusiasts have reported various experiences with The Squeaky Wheel, often noting its eerie and enigmatic nature. Many listeners have attempted to decode potential messages, fostering a community around these mysterious broadcasts.

Recent Activity

In recent years, The Squeaky Wheel has maintained regular transmissions, continuing to intrigue the radio community and spark discussions about its potential implications in military communications.

Recent updates (oddities)

On 17th August, 2023, The Squeaky Wheel changed the marker to "low-tone pip". Along with the marker change, they got a terrible microphone, but it was fixed recently in 2024.
